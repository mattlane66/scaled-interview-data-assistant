## Snapshot

- Tipping: Three months of research, aversion to mattress stores, distrust of a Groupon-style online purchase, then an unexpected Costco encounter with memory foam options at an acceptable price.
- Path X: Buy the softer, more expensive memory foam option at Costco for $699.
- Baseline result: UNKNOWN from this transcript alone; the interview captures the purchase decision and intended improvement more clearly than post-purchase outcomes.
- After-effects: Immediate relief that the purchase finally happened; wife became supportive once she felt the sample and price/risk seemed acceptable.

## Forces

- Pull: Hope of a good night's sleep, better next-day functioning, memory foam as a promising path, Costco as a trusted nearby retailer.
- Anxiety: Fear of repeating a bad mattress purchase, distrust of mattress stores, distrust of buying online through Groupon if returns became difficult.
- Inertia: Building a company, raising two young kids, moving homes, family logistics, and the hassle of making time for a mattress purchase.

## Job story
WHEN I keep waking up sore from a mattress that is sinking,
AND I am trying to stay functional as a business owner, husband, and dad while life keeps pushing the replacement down the list,
I WANT a low-risk way to replace the mattress with something that will help me sleep and wake up feeling normal,
SO I CAN be productive, less cranky, and more present the next day.

4. Must fit into a crowded real-life moment instead of requiring a high-friction shopping process.
5. Must be acceptable to both spouses quickly enough to act.
